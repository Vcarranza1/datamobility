Enhanced Mobility

Federal and state-funded transportation programs designed to fill the gaps that traditional transit simply cannot reach — serving seniors, individuals with disabilities, and low-income communities across Northern Virginia.

The Challenge

Bridging the Mobility Gap

Traditional public transit — buses and rail — serves millions of commuters every day. But it wasn't designed for everyone. Seniors who can't walk to a bus stop, veterans with PTSD who struggle in crowded spaces, individuals with disabilities who need door-to-door service — these populations fall through the cracks.

Enhanced Mobility programs exist to bridge that gap. Through a combination of FTA Section 5310 funding, state grants, and local matching funds, DATA Mobility Partners provides specialized transportation that goes where traditional transit cannot.

Our Programs

Enhanced Mobility Services

FTA Section 5310 Program

As a designated recipient of FTA Section 5310 funds, DATA coordinates enhanced mobility projects for seniors and individuals with disabilities. These funds support vehicle acquisition, mobility management, and direct service delivery across Northern Virginia.

Mobility Management

We serve as a regional mobility manager, helping individuals navigate the complex landscape of transportation options. Our mobility managers connect riders with the right service — whether it's paratransit, volunteer drivers, or our own programs.

First & Last Mile Solutions

We develop creative solutions for the "first and last mile" problem — connecting riders from their homes to the nearest transit hub through shuttle services, volunteer drivers, and on-demand micro-transit partnerships.

Grant Administration

We manage federal and state transportation grants on behalf of the region, ensuring compliance, maximizing utilization, and coordinating with partner agencies to stretch every dollar as far as possible.

Funding & Partners

How Enhanced Mobility Is Funded

Our Enhanced Mobility programs are made possible through a combination of federal, state, and local funding sources, along with partnerships with regional agencies.

Funding Sources

  • FTA Section 5310 — Enhanced Mobility of Seniors & Individuals with Disabilities
  • Virginia Department of Rail & Public Transportation (DRPT) grants
  • Fairfax County human services transportation funding
  • Private foundations and corporate sponsors
  • Individual donations and community fundraising

Regional Partners

  • Northern Virginia Transportation Commission (NVTC)
  • Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT)
  • Fairfax County Department of Neighborhood & Community Services
  • Loudoun County Area Agency on Aging
  • Prince William Area Agency on Aging
  • Local Veterans Affairs offices
FAQ

Common Questions

Enhanced Mobility programs primarily serve seniors (60+), individuals with disabilities, and low-income residents who lack access to reliable transportation. Eligibility varies by specific program, but our mobility managers can help determine which services you qualify for.
Most of our Enhanced Mobility services are provided at no cost to the rider. Some programs may request a voluntary suggested donation, but no one is ever turned away for inability to pay.
Our Enhanced Mobility programs currently serve residents of Fairfax County, Loudoun County, and Prince William County. Some programs extend to the broader Northern Virginia region. Contact us for specifics about coverage in your area.
Contact us by phone or email, and our mobility manager will walk you through the enrollment process. We'll assess your needs, explain available options, and help you get started with the right program. Most enrollments can be completed in a single phone call.
