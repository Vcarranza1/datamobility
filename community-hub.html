Community Hub

We serve seniors, veterans, and individuals with disabilities directly — getting them to medical appointments, grocery stores, social gatherings, and back into the world they helped build.

Our Mission

Every Ride Is a Lifeline

For many older adults and veterans in Northern Virginia, the loss of a driver's license — or the absence of nearby family — can mean near-total isolation. Medical appointments get missed. Groceries don't get bought. Social connections wither.

Our Community Hub exists to break that cycle. We provide safe, reliable, and dignified transportation that reconnects people with their communities and the essential services they depend on.

Programs

How We Serve Our Community

From group outings to individual medical rides, our programs are designed around the real needs of real people.

Venture Out Program

Group travel experiences that take seniors and veterans to farmers markets, museums, restaurants, and community events. These aren't just trips — they're social lifelines that combat isolation and bring joy.

Learn About Venture Out →

Medical Transportation

Reliable door-to-door rides to medical appointments, dialysis treatments, therapy sessions, and VA clinics. We ensure no veteran or senior misses critical healthcare because of a transportation barrier.

Cook for Vets

Volunteers prepare home-cooked meals and deliver them directly to homebound veterans. It's a program built on gratitude — nourishing both body and spirit for those who served our country.

Grocery & Essential Errands

Weekly transportation to grocery stores, pharmacies, banks, and other essential destinations. For seniors without reliable transportation, these rides mean the difference between self-sufficiency and dependence.

Real Impact

More Than Transportation

When Margaret, an 84-year-old widow living alone in Fairfax County, lost her ability to drive, she also lost her connection to the world. Her doctor's visits became impossible. Her weekly trip to the grocery store — gone. The isolation was devastating.

Through our Community Hub, Margaret now has reliable rides to every appointment, weekly grocery runs, and monthly Venture Out outings with friends she's made through the program. "They didn't just give me a ride," she says. "They gave me my life back."
